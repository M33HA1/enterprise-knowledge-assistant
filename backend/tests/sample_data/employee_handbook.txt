SECTION 1: PAID TIME OFF (PTO) POLICY

1.1 Annual PTO Allocation
All full-time employees receive the following PTO allocation based on tenure:
- 0–2 years of service: 15 days per year
- 3–5 years of service: 20 days per year
- 6–10 years of service: 25 days per year
- 10+ years of service: 30 days per year

PTO accrues monthly at a rate of 1/12th of the annual allocation.
Unused PTO may be carried over to the next year, up to a maximum of 5 days.

1.2 PTO Request Process
- Requests must be submitted at least 5 business days in advance via the HR portal.
- Requests of 5+ consecutive days require manager AND HR approval.
- PTO during blackout periods (Q4 close: Dec 15–31) requires VP-level approval.

1.3 Sick Leave
- Employees receive 10 sick days per year, separate from PTO.
- Sick leave of 3+ consecutive days requires a doctor's note.
- Unused sick leave does NOT carry over.

SECTION 2: REMOTE WORK POLICY

2.1 Eligibility
All employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period are eligible
for remote work, subject to manager approval and role requirements.

2.2 Remote Work Schedule
- Hybrid roles: minimum 3 days in-office per week (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday).
- Fully remote roles: must be approved by department VP.
- All employees must be available during core hours: 10:00 AM – 4:00 PM EST.

2.3 Home Office Requirements
- Employees must maintain a dedicated workspace with:
  - Reliable high-speed internet (minimum 50 Mbps)
  - Company-approved security software installed
  - Webcam and microphone for video meetings
- The company provides a one-time $500 home office stipend for new remote workers.

2.4 Remote Work from Different States/Countries
- Working from a different state for more than 30 days requires HR notification
  for tax compliance purposes.
- International remote work requires prior approval from Legal and HR.
- Maximum international remote work period: 90 days per calendar year.

SECTION 3: EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY

3.1 Eligible Expenses
The following expenses are reimbursable with proper documentation:
- Business travel (flights, hotels, ground transportation)
- Client entertainment (pre-approved, max $150 per person)
- Professional development (conferences, courses — max $2,000/year)
- Office supplies for remote workers (max $200/quarter)

3.2 Reimbursement Process
1. Submit expense report within 30 days of the expense.
2. Attach original receipts or digital copies.
3. Manager approval required for all expenses.
4. Finance processes reimbursements bi-weekly on the 1st and 15th.

3.3 Travel Policy
- Flights: book economy class for domestic; business class for flights over 6 hours.
- Hotels: maximum nightly rate of $200 domestic, $300 international.
- Meals: per diem of $75/day domestic, $100/day international.
- Rental cars: mid-size or below unless approved by manager.

SECTION 4: CODE OF CONDUCT

4.1 Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues with respect and dignity
- Maintain confidentiality of company information
- Report conflicts of interest to their manager or HR
- Follow all applicable laws and regulations

4.2 Anti-Harassment Policy
ACME Corporation maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment of any kind.
This includes but is not limited to:
- Sexual harassment
- Racial or ethnic discrimination
- Bullying or intimidation
- Retaliation against employees who report violations

Reports can be made to HR directly or via the anonymous Ethics Hotline: 1-800-555-ETHICS.
All reports are investigated within 5 business days.

4.3 Disciplinary Process
Violations of company policy follow a progressive discipline process:
1. Verbal warning (documented)
2. Written warning
3. Performance improvement plan (30–60 days)
4. Suspension without pay
5. Termination

Severe violations (theft, violence, fraud) may result in immediate termination.

SECTION 5: INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY
Department: IT

5.1 Password Requirements
- Minimum 12 characters
- Must include: uppercase, lowercase, numbers, special characters
- Passwords must be changed every 90 days
- No reuse of the last 10 passwords
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all systems

5.2 Data Classification
- PUBLIC: Marketing materials, press releases
- INTERNAL: Company policies, organizational charts
- CONFIDENTIAL: Financial data, customer information, employee records
- RESTRICTED: Trade secrets, M&A data, board communications

5.3 Acceptable Use of Technology
- Company devices are for business use primarily
- Limited personal use is permitted during non-work hours
- Prohibited: installing unauthorized software, accessing inappropriate content,
  using company resources for personal business ventures
- All company communications may be monitored in accordance with applicable law

5.4 Data Breach Response
In the event of a suspected data breach:
1. Immediately notify IT Security at security@acme.com
2. Do NOT attempt to investigate or contain the breach yourself
3. Preserve all evidence — do not delete files or logs
4. IT Security will initiate the Incident Response Plan within 1 hour
5. Affected parties will be notified within 72 hours per regulatory requirements
